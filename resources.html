Resources

Plain-English bookkeeping topics for small-business owners.

These resource summaries help owners understand what clean books should support before they choose a monthly bookkeeping or cleanup engagement.

Monthly close checklist

Bank and card activity should be reconciled, unclear transactions reviewed, receivables and payables checked, and reports prepared on a consistent schedule.

Learn about monthly bookkeeping

When cleanup is needed

Cleanup may be needed when reports do not make sense, accounts have not been reconciled, or tax-time preparation keeps revealing old bookkeeping issues.

Learn about cleanup

What reports should show

Useful reports help owners understand cash, receivables, expenses, profitability, and the routines that need follow-up.

Learn about reporting
Owner questions

Questions worth asking each month.

Good bookkeeping gives owners a short list of answers they can act on, rather than just a completed set of categories.

  • Do bank and credit card balances reconcile?
  • Which invoices are overdue?
  • Which bills are coming due?
  • Which expenses changed meaningfully?
  • Does profitability look different from last month?
  • What bookkeeping items still need owner input?
